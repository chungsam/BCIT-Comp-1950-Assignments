Book Recommendation

Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise
By Anders Ericsson and Robert Pool

Ever feel a sense of wonder and awe of how some people display "super-human" abilities and think "I'd never be able to do something like that!"?

Well, after reading this book, you'll come to realize that with enough time, perseverance and "deliberate" practice, you can learn (basically) anything you want!

Anders Ericsson shares his decades-worth of research of how violin virtuosos, star athletes, and memory champions do seemingly impossible feats. He demystifies the whole "having the right genes" myth and teaches us how to learn and practice the right way. He shares the secrets of how virtuosos get to where they get. And the good news for all of us, Ericsson suggests, is that there is no secret to all this--we just have to put in the right amount of hours, and put in the right kind of "deliberate" practice.

In essence, reading this book is "learning how to learn".

Here are some memorable quotes:

"A world in which deliberate practice is a normal part of life would be one in which people had more volition and satisfaction."
“The reason that most people don’t possess these extraordinary physical capabilities isn’t because they don’t have the capacity for them, but rather because they’re satisfied to live in the comfortable rut of homeostasis and never do the work that is required to get out of it. They live in the world of “good enough.” The same thing is true for all the mental activities we engage in.”
“A world in which deliberate practice is a normal part of life would be one in which people had more volition and satisfaction.”

Enjoy!

CSS3 Feature 1

Rotate

The rotate attribute allows you to rotate the element by x degrees.

Here, I've animated the quote icon by defining custom keyframes "rotate" animation that starts from 0 degrees and ends at 360 degrees: 

@keyframes rotate {
    from {
        transform: rotate(0deg);
    }
    to {
        transform: rotate(360deg);
    }
}

Supported on all major browsers.

CSS3 Feature 2

Repeating radial gradient

In addition to linear gradients, CSS3 now offers a radial gradient, which you can repeat to create some cool patterns.

Here's the CSS snippet: 

#el4{
    background:repeating-radial-gradient(
        #b8e7bf, 
        #b8e7bf 5px, 
        white 5px, 
        white 10px);
}

Supported on all major browsers.
